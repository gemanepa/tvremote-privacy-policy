Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 11, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how TV Remote WiFi handles information when you use the app to discover and control TVs and streaming devices on your local Wi-Fi network.

TV Remote WiFi does not require an account, does not collect your email address, phone number, contacts, Wi-Fi network name, precise location, or advertising identifiers, and does not sell personal information. The app uses your local network to find compatible TV devices and uses Firebase Crashlytics for crash reporting and diagnostics.

Information The App Handles

The app handles the following information to provide remote-control features:

  • Local network device information: device name, IP address, port, platform type, discovery source, and related local discovery metadata for TVs and streaming devices found through mDNS and SSDP.
  • Connection information stored on your device: the last connected TV device, whether the app should try to power it on, remote-control preferences, haptic preference, theme preference, and whether certain in-app tips have already been shown.
  • Platform pairing credentials stored on your device: credentials such as LG or Samsung client keys or tokens when needed to reconnect to a TV that you approved.
  • Crash and diagnostic information: crash reports, unhandled JavaScript exceptions, device and operating-system diagnostic context, and the limited custom diagnostic fields described below.

How Local Network Discovery Works

TV Remote WiFi uses your Wi-Fi/local network to discover compatible TVs and streaming devices. This discovery happens on your local network so the app can show available devices and send remote-control commands after you choose a device.

The app does not collect or send your Wi-Fi network name, Wi-Fi password, router information, precise location, contacts, or account data. Local device information is used to connect to and control devices on the same network.

Crashlytics Diagnostics

TV Remote WiFi uses Firebase Crashlytics, provided by Google, to understand crashes and reliability problems. Crash reports may be uploaded to Firebase after a crash or on the next app launch.

The app adds only limited diagnostic context to Crashlytics:

  • Current route, such as /connect, /settings, or /.
  • Connection status, such as idle, searching, selecting, pairing, or connected.
  • Connected platform type, such as androidtv, roku, samsung, lg, or none.
  • Discovery status and the number of discovered devices.
  • Remote command diagnostic logs containing command name, platform type, and whether the command succeeded or failed.

The app intentionally does not send TV names, TV IP addresses, pairing codes, stored platform credentials, raw discovery payloads, user identifiers, email addresses, phone numbers, contacts, Wi-Fi network names, full device objects, or request/response payloads to Crashlytics.

How Information Is Used

The app uses information for these purposes:

  • Discovering compatible TVs and streaming devices on your local network.
  • Connecting to the device you select.
  • Sending remote-control commands, such as navigation, volume, media, and app commands.
  • Remembering app settings and the last connected TV when you enable those behaviors.
  • Maintaining approved TV sessions so you do not need to pair again every time.
  • Diagnosing crashes and improving reliability.

Sharing And Third Parties

TV Remote WiFi does not sell personal information and does not use third-party advertising SDKs. Crash and diagnostic information is processed by Firebase Crashlytics, a Google service, to provide crash reporting. Google may process this information according to its own terms and privacy policies.

Data Stored On Your Device

Connection details, approved platform credentials, and app preferences are stored locally on your device. This local storage helps the app reconnect to TVs and remember your preferences. You can clear this local data by changing available in-app settings where provided or by uninstalling the app.

Permissions

The app may request local network permissions so it can discover and connect to TVs and streaming devices on your Wi-Fi network. If you deny or disable local network access, device discovery and remote-control features may not work.

Data Retention

Local app data remains on your device until you clear it, disable related settings, or uninstall the app. Crashlytics diagnostic data is retained by Firebase according to Firebase Crashlytics retention settings and policies.

Children's Privacy

TV Remote WiFi is not directed to children under 13. The app does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

International Processing

Crash and diagnostic information processed by Firebase Crashlytics may be processed in countries other than your own, depending on Google's infrastructure and service practices.

Changes To This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. When it changes, the effective date at the top of this page will be updated.
